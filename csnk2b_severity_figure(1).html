Figure. Clinical phenotypic spectrum of CSNK2B-related epilepsy. (A) Each row represents one patient (n=88 with available onset age). Horizontal bar = onset age (months, max displayed 120 mo); colored squares = seizure type(s) present; red circle = status epilepticus; orange square = drug-resistant epilepsy; rightmost column = total ASMs trialed. Rows sorted ascending by onset age; hover for variant. (B) Seizure type frequency across all 103 patients stratified by drug response. (C) ASM usage frequency (top 10 by total use), stratified by patient drug-response category. Abbreviations: GTC, generalized tonic-clonic; SE, status epilepticus; R, drug-resistant; ASM, antiseizure medication; VPA, valproate; LEV, levetiracetam; LTG, lamotrigine; OXC, oxcarbazepine; TPM, topiramate; ZNS, zonisamide; PB, phenobarbital; CLB, clobazam; PHT, phenytoin; KD, ketogenic diet.
